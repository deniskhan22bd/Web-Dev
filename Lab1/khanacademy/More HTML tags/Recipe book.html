Recipe #2 - Plov

  • Time: 2 hours, 30 minutes
  • Serves: 10
Ingredients Quantity
Rice 1 kg
Beef 1 kg
Carrot 1 kg
Onion 500 g
Vegetable oil 300 g

Step 1: Rinse the rice for Uzbek pilaf in a cauldron with cold water, changing it several times until the last water becomes completely clear. The rice is ready.

Step 2: Wash and cut lamb for pilaf into cubes. Peel 3 onions and the entire carrot. Slice the onion into thin half-rings and the carrot into long sticks with a thickness of 1 cm. Peel the garlic without separating the cloves.

Step 3: Heat the cauldron, pour in oil, and heat it until a light smoke appears. Add the remaining onion and fry it well until it turns dark golden. Remove the onion from the cauldron.

Step 4: Prepare the "zirvak" (base for Uzbek pilaf). Put the sliced onion into the cauldron and, stirring, fry it until it becomes dark golden for 5-7 minutes. Make sure it does not burn.

Step 5: Add the sliced lamb to the onion. Stirring with a spatula, fry the zirvak ingredients until they have a brownish crust. This may take about 10–15 minutes.

Step 6: Place the cauldron with the meat and onion to the side, add the carrot. Fry without stirring for 3 minutes. Then mix all the contents of the cauldron and cook for another 10 minutes, stirring occasionally with a spatula.

Step 7: Prepare the spice mix by crushing cumin and coriander with your fingers or a pestle. Add it to the zirvak along with barberries and salt to taste. You can add a bit more salt than you're used to since the rice will absorb some.

Step 8: Reduce the heat to medium and cook until the carrots become soft, 7–10 minutes. Pour 2 cm of boiling water into the cauldron. Add a hot pepper. Reduce the heat and stew the zirvak for 1 hour.

Step 9: Rinse the rice again, let the water drain. Spread the rice evenly over the zirvak in the cauldron, increase the heat to the maximum, and pour boiling water through a colander into the cauldron to cover the rice with a 3 cm layer.

Step 10: Once all the water is absorbed, press garlic cloves into the rice (halfway). Reduce the heat to medium and cook until the pilaf is ready under a lid.

Step 11: Make holes in the pilaf by tapping the rice lightly with a spoon. If the sound is dull, make several punctures with a thin stick to the very bottom. This allows steam to escape through the holes.

Step 12: Cover the cauldron with a large flat plate and a lid, reduce the heat to a minimum, and leave the pilaf on the heat for 30 minutes.

Recipe #3 - Pork

  • Time: 1 hour, 30 minutes
  • Serves: 10
Ingredients Quantity
Pork 2 kg
Garlic 6 cloves
Spices to your taste

Step 1: Prepare the pork for roasting. Wash it and pat it dry with paper towels. Peel the garlic cloves. Cut large cloves into long slices, leave small ones whole.

Step 2: Prepare the spice mix for the pork. Place sweet paprika, juniper, rosemary, cloves, coriander, and chili flakes in a mortar. Crush with a pestle. Add oil and mix well.

Step 3: Lay the pork on the table. Make deep incisions in the meat with a thin knife. Insert half of the garlic cloves into the incisions. Flip the meat to the other side and pierce it with the remaining garlic.

Step 4: Rub the pork with salt, then coat it with the spicy oil mixture from all sides. Wrap it in plastic wrap and leave it at room temperature for 1–1.5 hours.

Step 5: Preheat the oven to 200°C. Remove the plastic wrap from the pork and wrap the meat in foil. Place it on a baking sheet and roast for 1.5 hours. Serve hot.
